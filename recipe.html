Nigeria Jollof Rice

Although Jollof rice is a beloved West African classic dish, Nigerian jollof is termed the best. This aromatic rice dish, simmered in a rich tomato-based sauce with spices, works perfectly for both casual weeknight dinners and special occasions. The traditional preparation uses long-grain rice, enhanced with Nigerian curry powder and dried thyme. When paired with sweet fried plantains (known locally as dodo) and coleslaw, it creates a complete and satisfying meal. Source

In Nigeria it takes center stage as the main course! It is called Party Rice, gets its distinctive character from being prepared over open flames, creating layers of smoky depth and complex flavors that make it truly extraordinary.

Recipe Information

Prep time: 20 minutes

Cook time: 45 - 55 minutes

Servings: 6

Difficulty: Beginner - Intermediate

Recipe Source

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice, rinsed until water runs clear
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped (divide: half for stew, half for frying)
  • 4 ripe tomatoes or 1 can (400g) plum tomatoes, blended
  • 1 red bell pepper, blended
  • 2-3 scotch bonnet or habanero peppers, blended (to taste)
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 3 tbsp neutral oil (e.g., vegetable or sunflower)
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp dried thyme; 1 tsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika; 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (optional: peas, carrots)
  • Fresh parsley or spring onions for garnish

Instructions

  1. Blend the base: Combine tomatoes, red pepper, and scotch bonnet into a smooth purée; set aside.
  2. Build the stew: Heat oil over medium. Sauté half the chopped onion until translucent. Stir in tomato paste and cook for 3-4 minutes to remove raw taste.
  3. Season: Add the blended purée, thyme, curry powder, smoked paprika, bay leaves, and a pinch of salt. Simmer 10-12 minutes, stirring to prevent burning.
  4. Rice + stock: Pour in stock and bring to a gentle boil. Add the rinsed rice, stir once, then reduce heat to low. Cover tightly with a lid (or foil under the lid) to trap steam.
  5. Steam, don't stir: Cook 20-25 minutes on low heat. Avoid stirring, which can break the grains. If needed, add a splash of water around the edges.
  6. Finish: Fold in mixed vegetables (if using) and the remaining onions. Cover and cook 5-8 more minutes until rice is tender and fluffy.
  7. Rest: Turn off heat and let sit, covered, 5 minutes. Remove bay leaves.
  8. Serve: Fluff with a fork. Garnish with chopped parsley or spring onions. Adjust salt and pepper, then enjoy with your favorite protein.

Tips & Notes

  • For the signature smoky flavor, let a few grains toast lightly at the bottom but avoid burning.
  • Rinse rice thoroughly to prevent gumminess. Parboiled long-grain gives the best texture.
  • If heat sensitive, seed the scotch bonnet or use less; you can add heat later with chili flakes.
  • Keep the lid closed, steam cooks the rice evenly.
